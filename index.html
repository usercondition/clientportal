Services

What we take on

Each line below opens a fuller description — built for scanning on mobile and reading in depth on desktop without three identical cards.

Custom miniature printing

Infantry, monsters, and vehicles in common tabletop scales. We review supports, wall thickness, and base contact so fragile poses survive shipping — and we flag risky geometry before resin hits the vat.

  • Supported and hollowed builds where it saves weight and cost
  • Scale-consistent batches for army projects
  • Optional primer-ready finish notes on request

Bits, weapons & accessories

Conversion-friendly components: weapons, heads, pauldrons, scenic bases. Parts are laid out for reliable casting-like results from resin — including sprue or bagging preferences when you have a fulfillment workflow.

  • Thin features reviewed for printability
  • Packaging that matches how you sell or bundle

Prototypes & engineering parts

Fit prototypes, brackets, housings, and test jigs. We care about function: tolerances you can measure, surfaces that mate predictably, and clear communication when a design needs a revision pass.

  • Dimensional intent discussed up front
  • Iteration-friendly quoting when files evolve

File prep & CAD assistance

Not everyone ships print-ready STLs day one. We can help with orientation, support strategy, minor mesh repair, and build layout — always with an eye toward what actually works in resin, not just what passes a slicer check.

  • Honest feasibility notes before you commit
  • Clear handoff when you want to take files internal

Small-batch production

Short runs for indie launches, event stock, or club orders. Scheduling, resin lot consistency, and QC sampling are discussed up front so you know what “small batch” means in calendar time and unit count.

  • Repeatable packing for fulfillment partners
  • Lot notes available for color-matched reorders where applicable

How it works

From files to finished prints

A predictable path so you always know what happens next — no mystery queue, no jargon-heavy status pages.

  • 1

    Send files & intent

    STLs or project notes through the quote form or client portal. Include scale, quantity, material preference, and any hard deadlines.

  • 2

    Review & plan

    We confirm printability, support strategy, and pricing. If something will not survive shipping or needs a redesign, we say so before you pay.

  • 3

    Print & QC

    Parts are washed, cured, and inspected under good light. Critical fits get a quick physical check when the job demands it.

  • 4

    Pack & ship

    Padding and orientation chosen for thin spears, wings, and vehicle panels. Tracking shared when your parcel leaves the bench.

  • 5

    Follow-up

    Questions after delivery go through the same portal thread — useful for batch two planning or fit adjustments on prototypes.

Materials & standards

What “good” means here

We print for hobbyists who paint for hours and designers who measure in tenths — so we keep notes honest and tolerances explicit.

Resolution & equipment

High-resolution masked SLA workflows suited to fine cloak folds, lens surfaces, and crisp panel lines. Layer height and exposure are tuned per resin and season — not copied from a generic profile sheet.

Typical use
28–32mm figures, vehicle kits, detailed bits
File formats
STL preferred; STEP/OBJ by arrangement for engineering parts

Resins & handling

Standard tough and rigid formulations for most tabletop and mechanical prototypes. Specialty resins when the job truly needs them — we will not upsell a material your geometry cannot benefit from.

Curing & wash
Consistent wash cycles and post-cure so parts reach stable dimensions before packing
QC
Visual pass on every piece; spot checks on critical dimensions when specified
